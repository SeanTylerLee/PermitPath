Oversize Routing & Permit Routing Guide

Oversize routing and permit routing is about following the exact path your state permits require. For heavy-haul and oversize loads, the route on the permit is the law—deviating can mean fines, delays, or worse. This guide explains what oversize routing is and how PermitPath helps you stay on your permit-approved route.

What is Oversize Routing?

Oversize routing is the process of planning a path for a load that exceeds legal size or weight limits. Instead of just “shortest” or “fastest,” oversize routing must respect bridge limits, lane widths, curfews, construction, and state-by-state rules. The route is usually specified on your oversize or overweight permit, and you are expected to follow that permit routing exactly.

What is Permit Routing?

Permit routing is the route printed on the state permit itself. States may specify highways, exits, turn-by-turn directions, mile markers, or written instructions like “follow I‑80 WB to Exit 123, then US‑30 WB.” When you are hauling an oversize or overweight load, permit routing becomes your official navigation plan. Your job is to match your real‑world driving to that permit routing.

Why Oversize & Permit Routing Matters

Missing a turn on an oversize permit route can push you onto roads that are too low, too narrow, or not legal for your weight or dimensions. That can lead to:

  • Tickets and fines for being off your permit routing
  • Delays while you work with the permit office to correct the route
  • Safety risks around low bridges, tight turns, and restricted roads

A dedicated oversize routing app like PermitPath helps you stay locked onto the permit route from start to finish.

How PermitPath Helps with Oversize Routing

Best Practices for Oversize Routing

  • Always read the permit first and highlight the official route
  • Enter the same start, end, and major waypoints into PermitPath
  • Double‑check low bridges, weight limits, and curfews before you roll
  • Keep your permit and the app in sync—edit the route if the permit changes
